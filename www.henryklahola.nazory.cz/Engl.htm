The Memorare.

The Memorare has long been attributed to St. Bernard of Clairvaux (1090-1153), Confessor, Abbot, and Doctor of the Church. While some of St. Bernard's writings do indeed echo the words of the Memorare, modern scholarship indicates that he did not in fact compose it. The piece rather appears to be a 16th century version of a 15th century prayer. It was Claude Bernard (1588-1641) who first popularized the idea that St. Bernard composed it. A partial indulgence is granted to the faithful who recite the Memorare.
